Poetry by Serene Brielle

Dear Gemini
I look our daughter differently today
She has your little brown eyes
hiding white lies
And those two toned lips
That frown when I say your name
Because you were a light switch
Only I couldn’t control you

On a Monday you bought me Irises
My favorite flower
The heading of our lane
And my name
But your compassion died quicker
Than a moth to a flame

It was only five months
A few fights
22 weeks
Some sleepless nights
152 days
And in despite
Of the 3,648 hours
I spent
The extent
Of trying to prevent
I was hellbent
On fixing the fragments
Of a broken promise

It was only twelve months
After the riverfront wedding
That I told you I was expecting
And in the span of two seconds
I watched your whole life crumble
Your hands clenched in a fist
You continued to mumble
5 scathing slurred syllables
Suddenly the fate of our future
Wasn’t so subtle

On a Tuesday my daughter was born
My delicate lotus rising from the mud
Her heart had a steady thud
And you were lovelorn

The irritation I felt throughout my body was nothing
compared to the friction in our relationship
Which sparked so many fires
That we both burned out
Ashes in a dark pit

We were Pompeii
And you were an eruption of clay
Suffocating us in a coat of scorching anger
And unpredictable rage

But
A day of happiness, a day of pain
Soon forgotten like the passing rain
Not willing to make a change for good
You did what was comfortable, what you understood

The world is a bitter, cold place
So I thank you for giving us a taste
Of what life would be
If my daughter and I hadn’t broke free
From the chains of instability

Sincerely,
